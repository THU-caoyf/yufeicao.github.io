Our lab develops integrated strategies that connect **experiments, catalysis, and computation**. We are interested in both fundamental mechanisms and practical applications.

## Enzyme–Chemical Hybrid Catalysis

We aim to merge the selectivity of enzymes with the versatility of chemical catalysts. Current interests include cascade catalysis, cooperative catalysis, catalyst integration, and system-level optimization for complex transformations.

## Rational Enzyme Design

We apply AI-assisted design, molecular dynamics, QM/MM, docking, and structure-guided engineering to redesign enzymes for improved activity, selectivity, stability, and substrate scope.

## Enzyme Catalysis in Confined Spaces

We investigate catalytic mechanisms under confinement, including porous materials, interfaces, and nanoscale environments. A key question is how spatial restriction reshapes transport, structure, and reactivity.

## Food Detection and Food Big Data Analytics

We combine analytical chemistry, sensing, computational modeling, and data science to support food safety, quality assessment, molecular detection, and large-scale food data analysis.

## Representative Keywords

Biocatalysis, hybrid catalysis, enzyme engineering, electrocatalysis, molecular simulation, QM/MM, computational chemistry, computational biology, food sensing, and food informatics.
